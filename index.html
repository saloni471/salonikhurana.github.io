About

Who benefits from digital change —
and who gets left behind?

I am an Economist at the World Bank's Digital and AI Vice Presidency, Washington D.C., and a PhD Scholar in Economics at the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT), New Delhi (thesis submitted; expected June 2026).

I study how digital skills, AI, and technological change reshape employment, wages, and inequality in developing economies. My work spans labor economics, digital development, applied microeconometrics, and the political economy of trade — with a focus on translating large-scale data into actionable evidence for policy.

I have led country and regional analytics across South Asia, MENA, East Asia, Eastern Europe, and Africa. I also founded Data with Saloni, a mentoring initiative supporting early-career researchers on empirical methods and data analysis.
